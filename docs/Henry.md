## Vision

Henry exists to close the advice gap for America's 27 million HENRY households — High Earners, Not Rich Yet. These are people earning $150K–$500K who are too complex for budgeting apps, too early for private wealth managers, and too busy to build their own spreadsheets. They earn in the top 2–3% but don't feel wealthy. 77% lose sleep over money. 36% live paycheck to paycheck despite six-figure salaries.

The problem isn't income. It's clarity.

Henry gives them clarity. One app that answers the only question that matters:

> **"Given everything about my financial life, what should I do next?"**

## The Problem

HENRYs face a specific set of financial problems that no existing product solves well:

### 1. The Cash Flow Mystery
They earn $200K+ but don't know where it goes. Lifestyle creep absorbs every raise. They save 8–12% when they need 15–20%. They're 30% more likely to underestimate their spending than the average household. They don't need a budgeting app — they need a high-altitude view: how much is coming in, how much is going out, and what's actually left for building wealth.

### 2. No Financial Scoreboard
They have no idea if they're on track. They compare themselves to friends who lie about money and articles written for median-income households. Their net worth is mostly home equity and 401(k) — illiquid and hard to evaluate. They need a clear signal: on track, at risk, or behind — benchmarked against HENRYs like them.

### 3. Decision Paralysis on Major Life Moves
The most common posts on r/HENRYfinance (184K members): *"Can I afford a $1.6M house on $450K?"* / *"When can I quit my $600K job?"* / *"$400K cash coming in — where should it go?"* These are high-stakes, low-frequency decisions where the wrong choice costs $50K–$500K. There's no good tool for modeling the impact on their specific financial trajectory.

### 4. Tax Complexity
Effective rates approach 40%+. The $10K SALT cap punishes them. Phase-outs eliminate credits they should qualify for. Most don't execute backdoor Roth, mega backdoor Roth, HSA investing, or tax-loss harvesting — strategies worth $10K–$30K/year. They file taxes but don't do tax *planning*.

### 5. Equity Compensation Confusion
For tech HENRYs, RSUs are the largest component of compensation — and the most mismanaged. Withholding at 22% when actual rates are 32–37%. Concentration risk with 40%+ of net worth in employer stock. They don't understand the tax implications of hold vs. sell. One bad RSU strategy can cost $50K–$200K.

### 6. Competing Priorities
Student loans, mortgage, childcare, 401(k), backdoor Roth, HSA, 529, emergency fund, taxable investing — every dollar has 5+ competing claims. Without a personalized framework, they make suboptimal allocation decisions that compound over decades.

### 7. The Advice Gap
Too complex for Mint. Too "poor" for Goldman Sachs. Financial advisors charge $5K–$15K/year or require $1M+ minimums. So they ask Reddit, Google, or friends — and get generic answers from people who don't know their situation.

## What Henry Does

Henry is an AI-powered financial advisor built for HENRYs. It provides four core capabilities:

### 1. The Scoreboard — "Where do I stand?"

A clear, at-a-glance picture of your financial health:

- **Net worth** broken down: liquid, retirement, home equity
- **Cash flow**: income after tax → fixed costs → discretionary → wealth building rate
- **Savings rate**: actual vs. what's needed to reach your goals
- **Status signal**: On Track / At Risk / Behind — benchmarked against HENRYs your age, income, and metro
- **Trend**: getting better or getting worse over time

Not transaction-level. Not "you spent $4.50 at Starbucks." The insight is: *"You're saving 11% of your income. You need 18% to retire at 55."*

### 2. The Trajectory — "Where am I headed?"

A Monte Carlo projection of your financial life — not a single stock, not a portfolio, but *your entire financial trajectory*:

- 10/20/30-year projections with confidence bands (10th–90th percentile)
- Key milestones: *"72% chance of reaching $2.5M by 55"*
- Retirement readiness: *"At your current pace, you can retire at 57 with $8,200/month in today's dollars"*
- Real-time adjustment: change your savings rate and watch the trajectory shift

This is the hero screen. The thing that brings people back.

### 3. The Decision Lab — "What happens if...?"

Model major life decisions against your trajectory before you make them:

- **"Buy a $750K house"** → retirement moves from 55 to 60, savings rate drops to 9%
- **"Max out backdoor Roth"** → adds $340K to retirement in today's dollars
- **"Take the new job at $180K"** → after tax and commute, net impact is $1,800/month
- **"Pay off student loans vs. invest"** → side-by-side comparison with break-even analysis

The decisions HENRYs actually face: housing, retirement timing, debt payoff, career changes, equity comp, education funding, windfalls, and savings changes. Quantified. Specific. Not vibes.

### 4. Sir Henry — "Talk me through this"

An AI advisor that knows your complete financial picture and speaks like a brilliant friend who happens to be a CFP:

- Ask anything in plain language: *"Should I sell my RSUs on vest?" / "Can I afford private school?" / "What should I do with a $50K bonus?"*
- Responds with your specific numbers — not generic advice
- Tax-aware: knows your bracket, flags missed opportunities, suggests strategies
- Equity comp literate: understands RSUs, ISOs, NSOs, ESPP
- Proactive: *"Your RSUs vest next month — at your marginal rate, that's a $14,200 tax event. The 22% withholding won't cover it."*

Sir Henry sounds like a smart friend — direct, specific, uses your numbers, never preachy, never condescending. The opposite of "consider consulting a financial professional."
