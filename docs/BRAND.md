## 1. Brand Identity

### Brand Name

**Henry** — always capitalized, never "HENRY" in consumer-facing copy (that's the acronym). The product is a person — a trusted guide.

- Product name: **Henry**
- AI advisor name: **Sir Henry**
- Legal / full: **Henry Financial, Inc.**

### Brand Essence

Henry is the financial advisor you never thought you could afford — built for people who earn well but haven't yet built real wealth. Confident, clear-eyed, and always on your side.

### Brand Personality

| Trait         | What it means                                                        | What it's NOT                        |
|---------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Confident     | Speaks with authority. Knows the numbers.                            | Arrogant, dismissive, salesy         |
| Clear         | No jargon. No hedging. Direct answers.                               | Dumbed-down, patronizing             |
| Sophisticated | Respects that our users are smart, high-earning professionals        | Stuffy, elitist, overly formal       |
| Warm          | Feels like a trusted friend who happens to be brilliant with money   | Cold, robotic, corporate             |
| Aspirational  | You're on a path. Henry helps you get there.                         | Preachy, guilt-tripping, fear-based  |

### Tagline

**Primary:** "The financial advisor you've been earning."

**Alternates:**
- "From high earner to genuinely wealthy."
- "Decisions, not budgets."
- "Your money. Your plan. Your Henry."

### Brand Promise

Henry gives you the clarity and confidence to make the financial decisions that actually matter — without needing a million dollars or a million spreadsheets.

**High Earners, Not Rich Yet**

## 3. Voice & Tone

### Voice (constant)

Henry always sounds like: **a smart friend who happens to be a financial expert.**

| Principle         | Example                                                                |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Direct            | "You can afford this house — but it'll push retirement back 3 years." |
| Honest            | "Your savings rate is solid. Your investment mix needs work."          |
| Conversational    | "Let's look at what happens if you max out your 401(k)."              |
| Empowering        | "Here are your options. Here's what I'd consider."                    |
| Never preachy     | NOT: "You really should be saving more."                               |

### Tone (varies by context)

| Context                      | Tone                          | Example                                                    |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Onboarding                   | Warm, welcoming, easy         | "Let's get the lay of the land. This takes about 5 minutes." |
| Dashboard / projections      | Confident, clear              | "You're on track to hit $1.2M by 55."                      |
| Negative news                | Honest, calm, solution-first  | "Market's down 12%. Your plan still works. Here's why."    |
| Major decisions              | Thoughtful, structured        | "Three things to consider before you buy."                  |
| Sir Henry (AI chat)          | Smart-casual, slightly witty  | "Good question. Short answer: yes, but let me show you why." |
| Error states                 | Helpful, never blaming        | "We couldn't pull that data. Try refreshing, or we'll fix it shortly." |
| Marketing / landing pages    | Bold, aspirational, concise   | "The financial advisor you've been earning."                |

### Language Rules

1. **Use "you" and "your"** — never "the user" or "one's portfolio"
2. **Use numbers** — "$1.2M by 55" not "a comfortable retirement"
3. **Avoid jargon** — "growth" not "alpha"; "mix of investments" not "asset allocation" (unless the user prefers technical terms)
4. **Be specific** — "3 years earlier" not "sooner"
5. **Never guilt** — We inform. We don't judge spending.
6. **Active voice** — "You'll reach $500K in 4 years" not "The target will be reached in 4 years"

## 4. Messaging Framework

### Hierarchy of Messages

**Level 1 — What is Henry? (5 seconds)**
> Henry is the AI financial advisor for people who earn well but aren't wealthy yet.

**Level 2 — What does it do? (15 seconds)**
> Henry runs 10–30 year projections of your financial life, helps you make major decisions (house, retirement, debt), and gives you AI-powered guidance personalized to your situation.

**Level 3 — Why is it different? (30 seconds)**
> Budgeting apps assume you don't know where your money goes. Robo-advisors give you a generic portfolio. Wealth managers won't talk to you under $1M. Henry sits in the gap — sophisticated enough for complex financial situations, accessible without a minimum balance.

### Key Messages by Audience Moment

| Moment                        | Message                                                                             |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| First impression (ad/landing) | "The financial advisor you've been earning."                                        |
| Considering signup             | "No minimums. No commissions. Just clarity."                                        |
| Onboarding                    | "Tell us where you are. We'll show you where you're going."                         |
| First projection               | "This is your financial future — in 10, 20, and 30 years."                          |
| Major decision                 | "Let's model this together. No guessing."                                           |
| Retention / re-engagement     | "Your plan has updated. Your retirement date moved up 6 months."                    |
| Referral                      | "Know someone stuck in the advice gap? Give them Henry."                            |

### Prohibited Messages

- "Track every dollar" (we're not a budgeting app)
- "Beat the market" (we don't trade)
- "Get rich quick" (we're about long-term planning)
- "Financial freedom" (overused, meaningless)
- "Set it and forget it" (we're active guidance, not passive)

## 5. Color System

### Design Philosophy

Henry uses a **dual-theme system** — light mode for approachability and marketing, dark mode for data-dense financial views. Inspired by the sophisticated dark interfaces of Fey and Robinhood for data, and the clean light presentations of Airwallex and Wise for marketing.

### Brand Colors

#### Primary: Henry Green

The signature color. Represents growth, wealth building, and forward momentum. Used sparingly for primary actions, key data points, and brand moments.

```
Henry Green
  Light:  #16A34A  (rgb 22, 163, 74)
  Dark:   #22C55E  (rgb 34, 197, 94)
  Subtle: #DCFCE7  (rgb 220, 252, 231)  — light mode backgrounds only
```

#### Secondary: Warm Gold

The aspirational accent. Hints at the wealth-building journey. Used for premium features, milestones, and positive financial moments.

```
Warm Gold
  Light:  #CA8A04  (rgb 202, 138, 4)
  Dark:   #EAB308  (rgb 234, 179, 8)
  Subtle: #FEF9C3  (rgb 254, 249, 195)  — light mode backgrounds only
```

#### Semantic Colors

```
Positive / On Track
  Light:  #16A34A
  Dark:   #22C55E

Warning / Attention
  Light:  #D97706
  Dark:   #F59E0B

Negative / Off Track
  Light:  #DC2626
  Dark:   #EF4444

Info / Neutral
  Light:  #2563EB
  Dark:   #3B82F6
```

### Light Theme

```

  Primary:    #FFFFFF   (pure white)
  Secondary:  #F9FAFB   (gray-50, cards, sections)
  Tertiary:   #F3F4F6   (gray-100, inputs, wells)
```

### Dark Theme

Inspired by Fey's rich dark palette — warm enough to feel premium, dark enough for extended data reading.

```
Background
  Primary:    #0A0A0B   (near-black, main background)
  Secondary:  #141416   (cards, panels)
  Tertiary:   #1C1C1F   (inputs, wells, hover states)

  Primary:    #F9FAFB   (gray-50, headings, body)
  Secondary:  #9CA3AF   (gray-400, supporting text)
  Tertiary:   #6B7280   (gray-500, placeholders, captions)

Borders
  Default:    #27272A   (zinc-800)
  Strong:     #3F3F46   (zinc-700)

Surfaces
  Card:       #141416   with 1px border #27272A
  Elevated:   #1C1C1F   with subtle border glow
  Modal:      #1C1C1F   with border #3F3F46, overlay #000000CC
```

### Color Usage Rules

1. **Henry Green is reserved** for primary CTAs, key positive metrics, and brand identity. Never use it for decorative backgrounds.
2. **Warm Gold** appears only for premium/milestone moments (e.g., "You hit $100K!" or Sir Henry's avatar). Max 1-2 gold elements per screen.
3. **Red is only for financial negatives** (losses, off-track) and destructive actions. Never for branding or attention-grabbing.
4. **Charts and graphs** use the full semantic palette. Series colors should be distinct and accessible (see Data Visualization below).
5. **Light mode is default** for marketing pages, onboarding, and settings. Dark mode is default for the dashboard, projections, and Sir Henry chat.
6. Both themes must meet **WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios** (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text/UI components).

## 6. Typography

### Design Philosophy

Clean, modern, highly readable at all sizes. Inspired by Wise's bold headlines and Fey's data-focused clarity. Two typeface families — one for personality (headings), one for precision (data and body).

### Typeface Stack

#### Primary: Inter

Used for body text, UI labels, data, and all functional text. Chosen for exceptional readability at small sizes, extensive weight range, and tabular number support.

```
Font family: 'Inter', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif
Weights:     400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold)
```

#### Display: Plus Jakarta Sans

Used for headings, marketing copy, and brand moments. Geometric, modern, and slightly warmer than Inter — gives Henry a distinctive personality without sacrificing readability.

```
Font family: 'Plus Jakarta Sans', 'Inter', sans-serif
Weights:     500 (Medium), 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold), 800 (ExtraBold)
```

#### Monospace: JetBrains Mono

Used exclusively for financial figures, percentages, projections, and tabular data. Ensures alignment and scannability in dense financial views.

```
Font family: 'JetBrains Mono', 'SF Mono', 'Fira Code', monospace
Weights:     400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 700 (Bold)
```

### Type Scale

Based on a 1.250 ratio (Major Third), optimized for financial readability.

```
Token            Size    Line Height  Weight              Typeface            Usage
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
display-xl       48px    1.1          800 ExtraBold       Plus Jakarta Sans   Landing hero
display-lg       36px    1.15         700 Bold            Plus Jakarta Sans   Section heroes
display-md       30px    1.2          700 Bold            Plus Jakarta Sans   Page titles
heading-lg       24px    1.3          600 SemiBold        Plus Jakarta Sans   Section titles
heading-md       20px    1.3          600 SemiBold        Plus Jakarta Sans   Card titles
heading-sm       16px    1.4          600 SemiBold        Inter               Subsection titles
body-lg          18px    1.6          400 Regular         Inter               Long-form text
body-md          16px    1.5          400 Regular         Inter               Default body
body-sm          14px    1.5          400 Regular         Inter               Secondary text
caption          12px    1.4          500 Medium          Inter               Labels, captions
overline         11px    1.3          600 SemiBold        Inter               ALL CAPS labels
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
money-lg         32px    1.2          700 Bold            JetBrains Mono      Hero balances
money-md         24px    1.2          600 Medium          JetBrains Mono      Card balances
money-sm         16px    1.3          500 Medium          JetBrains Mono      Inline amounts
money-xs         14px    1.3          400 Regular         JetBrains Mono      Table data
percent          14px    1.3          600 SemiBold        JetBrains Mono      Returns, rates
```

### Typography Rules

1. **Financial numbers always use JetBrains Mono** — dollar amounts, percentages, projections, dates in data contexts.
2. **Headings use Plus Jakarta Sans** — never for body text or data.
3. **Maximum 2 type sizes per card/component** — one for the title, one for content. Exception: data-dense tables.
4. **Letter spacing:** Overline labels use `+0.05em`. All other text uses default tracking.
5. **Number formatting:** Always use commas for thousands ($1,200,000), period for decimals. Currency symbol before number, no space.
6. **Tabular numbers:** Enable `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums` for all financial data to ensure column alignment.

## 8. Iconography & Imagery

### Icon Style

- **Style:** Outlined (not filled), 1.5px stroke, rounded caps and joins
- **Library:** Lucide Icons (primary) — consistent, open-source, extensive financial icon set
- **Size tokens:** 16px (inline), 20px (default), 24px (emphasis), 32px (feature)
- **Color:** Inherit from text color. Never decorative-colored unless semantic (green checkmark, red alert).

### Icon Usage

```
Navigation:        20px, secondary text color
Inline with text:  16px, inherit text color
Card headers:      24px, secondary text color
Feature sections:  32px, Henry Green on subtle green background (light) or dark surface (dark)
Empty states:      48px, tertiary text color
```

### Illustration Style

Henry uses **minimal, geometric illustrations** — not cartoons, not photographs.

- Clean lines, limited color (brand green, gold, grays)
- Abstract representations of financial concepts (growth curves, building blocks, paths)
- Never clip art, never stock photography of "happy people looking at laptops"
- Inspired by Midday's clean black-and-white aesthetic with selective color pops

### Photography (Marketing Only)

When used on landing pages or ads:

- Lifestyle photography of young professionals in natural settings
- Warm, natural lighting — never stock-photo-sterile
- Diverse representation mandatory
- Always desaturated slightly to not compete with UI
- Never used inside the product itself

### Chart & Graph Style

```
Line charts:
  - 2px stroke, rounded
  - Hover state: dot + tooltip
  - Grid lines: 1px, 10% opacity of text color
  - Axis labels: caption size, tertiary text color

Area / projection fans:
  - Gradient fills at 10-30% opacity
  - Crisp median line at 2px
  - Labeled confidence bands

Bar charts:
  - 8px border-radius on tops
  - 12px minimum bar width
  - 4px gap between grouped bars

Data labels:
  - Always use JetBrains Mono
  - Always right-align numbers
  - Green for positive, red for negative, no color for neutral
```

## 10. Platform Guidelines

### Shared Principles (All Platforms)

1. **Same core experience** — projections, decisions, Sir Henry work identically everywhere
2. **Responsive, not adaptive** — one design system, not three separate designs
3. **Touch targets: 44px minimum** on all interactive elements (Apple HIG + Material)
4. **System theme detection** — respect OS light/dark preference, allow manual override
5. **Offline-capable** — last-viewed projections cached, shown with "last updated" timestamp

### iOS Specific

```
Framework:       SwiftUI preferred, UIKit where needed
Navigation:      Tab bar (bottom), NavigationStack for drill-downs
Typography:      Use SF Pro as system fallback; prefer our type stack via custom fonts
Safe areas:      Respect all safe area insets (notch, home indicator, Dynamic Island)
Haptics:         Light haptic on milestone achievements, tab switches
Dark mode:       Follow system by default, in-app toggle
Status bar:      Light content on dark bg, dark content on light bg
```

### Android Specific

```
Framework:       Jetpack Compose preferred
Navigation:      Bottom Navigation (Material 3), top app bar for context
Typography:      Use Roboto as system fallback; prefer our type stack
Edge-to-edge:    Draw behind system bars, tint to match theme
Material You:    Support dynamic color theming optionally; brand colors take priority
Dark mode:       Follow system by default, in-app toggle
Gesture nav:     Respect predictive back gesture (Android 14+)
```

### Web Specific

```
Framework:       Next.js (React) with TypeScript
Styling:         Tailwind CSS with design tokens mapped to CSS custom properties
State:           Responsive sidebar + content layout
Breakpoints:     Mobile-first, progressive enhancement
Animations:      Framer Motion for page transitions, chart animations
SSR:             Marketing pages server-rendered; dashboard client-rendered
A11y:            ARIA labels on all interactive elements, keyboard navigable
```

### Cross-Platform Token Format

All design tokens should be maintained in a single source of truth and exported per platform:

```
tokens/
  colors.json       → Tailwind config, SwiftUI Color extensions, Compose Color
  typography.json    → Tailwind config, SwiftUI Font, Compose Typography
  spacing.json       → Tailwind config, SwiftUI padding, Compose Dp
  components.json    → shared component specs
```

## 11. Do's and Don'ts

### Brand Voice

| DO                                                    | DON'T                                                  |
|-------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| "You're projected to hit $1M by 52."                  | "Our algorithm calculates optimal wealth trajectories." |
| "Here's what I'd consider."                           | "You should do this."                                   |
| "Let's model this."                                   | "Click here to see your results!"                       |
| "Your plan still works."                              | "Don't panic!"                                          |
| Use real numbers and timeframes                       | Use vague feel-good language                            |

### Design

| DO                                                    | DON'T                                                  |
|-------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Use Henry Green for CTAs and key metrics              | Paint everything green                                 |
| Use dark mode for data-dense views                    | Use dark mode for onboarding or marketing              |
| Show confidence ranges on projections                 | Show single-number predictions without ranges          |
| Use monospace for all financial figures               | Mix sans-serif and monospace numbers in one component  |
| Provide white space — let data breathe               | Cram every metric onto one screen                      |
| Animate chart drawing on load                         | Use gratuitous animations that delay information       |
| Show "last updated" timestamps on financial data      | Show stale data without indication                     |

### Messaging

| DO                                                    | DON'T                                                  |
|-------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| "Decision engine for high earners"                    | "Budgeting app for millennials"                        |
| "AI-powered guidance"                                 | "Robo-advisor"                                         |
| "Personalized to your situation"                      | "One-size-fits-all recommendations"                    |
| Reference specific life decisions                     | Talk about "managing money" generically                |
| "No minimums. No commissions."                        | "Affordable" (implies cheap/low quality)               |

### Logo & Brand

| DO                                                    | DON'T                                                  |
|-------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| Use the wordmark "Henry" on light or dark backgrounds | Abbreviate to "H" except in the app icon               |
| Maintain minimum clear space around the logo          | Place the logo on busy backgrounds                     |
| Use the Warm Gold "H" monogram for the app icon       | Use Henry Green for the app icon (too similar to others)|
| Pair logo with tagline in marketing contexts          | Use tagline inside the product UI                      |
