## Detailed Competitor Analysis

### Category 1: AI Financial Advisors

#### Range + Rai
- **Price**: $145/month ($1,740/year)
- **What it does**: All-in-one wealth management platform. AI advisor "Rai" trained on 275+ years of combined advisor experience. Covers investments, taxes, retirement, cash flow, real estate, equity compensation, estate planning.
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive. Beautiful design. Strong press (Benzinga, NerdWallet). Rai AI understands cross-domain financial relationships. Human advisor access included.
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive ($1,740/year puts it in human-advisor territory). Requires onboarding call with a human. Not self-serve at entry. Tries to be everything for everyone — may overwhelm. Not specifically HENRY-positioned.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry is self-serve, focused on HENRYs specifically, priced for accessibility, and centers on the decision-making loop rather than wealth management.

#### Warren
- **Price**: Free tier + paid plans
- **What it does**: AI financial advisor that connects to your accounts. Gives personalized daily advice on saving, investing, taxes, and debt.
- **Strengths**: Low friction onboarding. Good conversational UX. Specific dollar recommendations. Free to start.
- **Weaknesses**: Broad audience (not HENRY-specific). Shallow analysis — doesn't do Monte Carlo projections or complex scenario modeling. No equity comp expertise. Generic advice at scale.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry is built for HENRY-level complexity (RSUs, backdoor Roths, $1M house decisions). Warren is more "financial wellness for everyone."

#### Cleo
- **Price**: Free tier + $5.99/month premium
- **What it does**: AI chatbot for money management. Tracks spending, suggests budgets, savings challenges. Fun/sassy tone.
- **Strengths**: Great UX, personality-driven, popular with younger users.
- **Weaknesses**: Budgeting-focused. Too basic for HENRYs. Doesn't handle projections, tax strategy, equity comp, or life decisions. Targets a younger, lower-income demographic.
- **Why Henry is different**: Completely different audience and problem set. Henry doesn't track spending or suggest savings challenges.

### Category 2: Retirement / Projection Tools

#### ProjectionLab
- **Price**: ~$80/year
- **What it does**: Financial projection tool with Monte Carlo simulations, scenario modeling, and beautiful visualizations. Manual data entry of income, expenses, accounts, and goals.
- **Strengths**: Beautiful UI. Excellent Monte Carlo implementation. Good scenario comparison. Active development. Loved by r/financialplanning community.
- **Weaknesses**: No AI — entirely manual. Steep learning curve. No tax strategy. No equity comp handling. Projection-only — doesn't tell you what to DO, just shows you outcomes. Time-consuming to set up.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry adds AI interpretation ("here's what this projection means for you"), tax-aware recommendations, equity comp support, and reduces the setup burden. ProjectionLab shows you the chart; Henry tells you what to do about it.

#### Boldin (formerly NewRetirement)
- **Price**: ~$120/year (PlannerPlus)
- **What it does**: Comprehensive retirement planning tool with detailed inputs for income, Social Security, taxes, healthcare, and estate planning. Monte Carlo simulations.
- **Strengths**: Very detailed. Good for people who want to model everything. Includes tax projections and Social Security optimization.
- **Weaknesses**: Feels like a spreadsheet with a GUI. Overwhelming number of inputs. Retirement-focused (not the full HENRY picture). No AI. No equity comp. UI feels dated. Learning curve is steep.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry is designed to be approachable, not exhaustive. AI handles the complexity so the user doesn't have to fill out 200 fields.

### Category 3: Robo-Advisors / Portfolio Management

#### Betterment
- **Price**: 0.25% of AUM
- **What it does**: Automated investing with tax-loss harvesting. Goal-based investing. Tax-coordinated portfolios.
- **Strengths**: Simple. Low-cost. Good for hands-off investors. Tax-loss harvesting is genuinely valuable.
- **Weaknesses**: Generic portfolios — no personalization beyond risk tolerance. No financial planning. No life decision modeling. No tax strategy beyond harvesting. Doesn't know your full picture.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry doesn't manage your money — it advises on your entire financial life. Betterment handles the portfolio; Henry handles the plan.

#### Wealthfront
- **Price**: 0.25% of AUM
- **What it does**: Similar to Betterment. Automated investing + financial planning tools. Direct indexing at $100K+.
- **Strengths**: Good financial planning features (Path tool). Direct indexing for tax efficiency. Clean UI.
- **Weaknesses**: Planning tools are basic compared to dedicated planning software. Still fundamentally a portfolio manager, not an advisor. Limited life-decision modeling.
- **Why Henry is different**: Similar differentiation to Betterment. Henry is the planning layer, not the portfolio layer.

#### Mezzi
- **Price**: $199/year
- **What it does**: AI-powered portfolio management for self-directed investors. Unified account view, rebalancing recommendations, tax-loss harvesting, wash sale prevention.
- **Strengths**: Excellent for portfolio-level insights. Tax-aware. Privacy-first. Good for high earners managing their own portfolios.
- **Weaknesses**: Portfolio-only focus. Doesn't help with life decisions, retirement planning, cash flow, or equity comp strategy. Not a financial planning tool.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry covers the full financial picture; Mezzi covers one piece of it (the portfolio). They could actually be complementary.

---

### Category 4: Free Aggregators

#### Empower (formerly Personal Capital)
- **Price**: Free (with upsell to human advisors at 0.89% AUM, $100K minimum)
- **What it does**: Account aggregation, net worth tracking, retirement planner, investment checkup, fee analyzer.
- **Strengths**: Free. Good account linking. Decent retirement planner. Large user base. Nice mobile app.
- **Weaknesses**: The free tool is a lead gen funnel for their wealth management business. Retirement planner is basic. No AI guidance. No decision modeling. No tax strategy. Feels increasingly stale.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry is the product, not the funnel. No upsell to a human advisor. AI guidance is the core, not a lead-gen hook.

---

### Category 5: Human Financial Advisors

#### Fee-Only CFPs
- **Price**: $5,000-$15,000/year (or $250-$500/hour)
- **What they do**: Comprehensive financial planning — cash flow, tax strategy, retirement, estate planning, insurance, equity comp, goal coordination. The full picture.
- **Strengths**: Truly personalized. Handles complexity. Behavioral coaching. Accountability. Can coordinate with your CPA and estate attorney.
- **Weaknesses**: Expensive. Slow (quarterly meetings). Hard to find a good one. Many still push AUM-based models. Availability limited to scheduled meetings. Not accessible to HENRYs with <$500K in investable assets.
- **Why Henry is different**: Henry provides ~80% of the planning value at ~5-10% of the cost. Available 24/7. No minimums. Instant scenario modeling. Not a replacement for CFPs on complex estate/tax situations, but handles the 80% of questions that don't require a licensed professional.

### The White Space

Nobody combines all of:
1. HENRY-specific framing and benchmarks
2. AI-powered conversational guidance
3. Monte Carlo projections on your *life* (not just a portfolio)
4. Major life decision modeling with quantified impact
5. Tax-aware recommendations
6. Equity comp guidance
7. Self-serve, accessible pricing

**This intersection is Henry's opportunity.**
