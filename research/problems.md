# The 7 Core Problems HENRYs Face

> Every product must solve a real problem. These are the validated, research-backed problems that Henry exists to address.

---

## Problem Ranking Criteria

Problems are ranked by:
1. **Frequency** — How often does this come up in research, forums, and advisor consultations?
2. **Severity** — How much does this cost the HENRY if left unsolved (in dollars and stress)?
3. **Solvability** — Can software meaningfully help with this, or does it require a human?

## Problem 1: The Cash Flow Mystery
**"Where does all my money go?"**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Very High |
| Severity | High ($10K-$50K/year in preventable wealth erosion) |
| Solvability | High |

### The Evidence
- HENRYs average **$68,000/year** in non-essential spending (travel, dining, shopping, premium services)
- Earners above $250K are **30% more likely to underestimate their annual spending** (Sequoia Financial, 2026)
- **36% of households earning $250K+** live paycheck to paycheck (LendingClub, 2024)
- **34% of households making $100K+** spend as much as they earn (Fortune, citing recent surveys)
- Average HENRY household saves only **8-12%** of take-home pay vs. the **15-20%** needed for on-track wealth building

### What's Actually Happening
Lifestyle creep is the silent killer. It's not that HENRYs are irresponsible — it's that every income increase gets absorbed by a slightly bigger house, a slightly nicer car, one more subscription, one more vacation. The baseline keeps rising. And because they don't budget (budgeting feels beneath them), they genuinely don't know where the money goes.

The result: a household earning $300K that saves the same percentage as a household earning $100K. The income advantage is wasted.

### What They Need (Not What They Think They Need)
They don't need a budgeting app. They need a **cash flow x-ray** — a high-altitude view:
- Total income (after tax)
- Fixed costs (housing, childcare, insurance, debt payments)
- Discretionary spending (everything else, as a lump)
- What's left = wealth building rate
- How that rate compares to what's needed for their goals

The insight isn't "you spent $47 at Starbucks." It's **"you're saving 11% of your income; you need 18% to retire at 55."**

## Problem 2: No Financial Scoreboard
**"Am I on track? How do I even know?"**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Very High |
| Severity | High (years of misdirected effort) |
| Solvability | Very High |

### The Evidence
- **77% of HENRYs lose sleep** over financial worries (Project Henry, 2025)
- **48%** expected to feel more comfortable on their salary than they do (Killik & Co, 2025)
- **25% aren't sure how their retirement savings are invested** (Invested With You, citing millennial HENRY data)
- Over **25% of households earning $200K-$300K** are unhappy with their finances (Killingsworth research, cited by AOL)
- Most net worth benchmarking is based on all Americans — HENRYs compare themselves to the wrong cohort

### What's Actually Happening
HENRYs have no dashboard for their financial life. They check their bank balance, maybe look at their 401(k) once a quarter, and occasionally update a spreadsheet. But they have no idea whether they're ahead, behind, or on track — because "on track" isn't defined for someone like them.

Comparison sources are toxic: friends (who exaggerate or understate), social media (fiction), generic articles ("the average 35-year-old has $X saved" — but HENRYs aren't average).

### What They Need
A **personalized scoreboard** with:
- Net worth (broken down: liquid / retirement / home equity / other)
- Savings rate (actual vs. required for their stated goals)
- A clear signal: on track / at risk / behind
- Benchmarking against HENRYs with similar age, income, and location
- Trend over time (are things getting better or worse?)

## Problem 3: Decision Paralysis on Major Life Moves
**"Can I afford [this major decision]? What will it cost me long-term?"**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | High (3-5 major decisions per HENRY per decade) |
| Severity | Very High ($50K-$500K+ impact per decision) |
| Solvability | High |

### The Evidence
From r/HENRYfinance (184K members), the most common posts are:
- "Can I afford a $1.6M house on $450K tech income?"
- "$400K cash coming in — where should I invest it?"
- "When can I quit my $600K job to pivot careers?"
- "Buying a vacation home with cash — talk me out of it"
- "Should I pay off student loans or invest?"
- "Wife wants to stop working — can we afford it on one income?"

These questions get dozens to hundreds of responses — from anonymous strangers who don't know the poster's full situation.

### What's Actually Happening
Major financial decisions are **high-stakes and low-frequency**. A HENRY might buy a house once or twice in their life, decide when to exercise stock options a few times, and plan retirement once. The wrong choice on any of these can cost $50K-$500K+.

But there's no good tool for modeling the impact. So they either:
1. Ask Reddit (strangers without context)
2. Pay a CFP $2,000-$5,000 for a one-time financial plan
3. Use a basic calculator that doesn't capture their full picture
4. Just guess and hope for the best

### What They Need
A **decision modeler** that takes their full financial picture and shows the impact of a specific decision:
- "Buy a $750K house with 20% down" → "Your savings rate drops from 18% to 9%. Retirement moves from age 55 to 60. 62% probability of reaching $2M."
- "Max out backdoor Roth annually" → "Adds $340K to your retirement balance in today's dollars."
- "Take the new job at $180K (from $150K)" → "After tax + new commute costs, net impact is $1,800/month."
- Side-by-side scenario comparison: Option A vs. Option B vs. Do Nothing

## Problem 4: Tax Complexity and Overpayment
**"I know I'm paying too much in taxes. I just don't know what to do about it."**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | High (annual, with peaks around equity comp events) |
| Severity | Very High ($5K-$30K+/year in missed optimization) |
| Solvability | Medium-High (guidance yes, execution requires action outside the app) |

### The Evidence
- Effective tax rates for HENRYs approach or exceed **40%** (federal 32-37% + state 6-13%)
- **$10K SALT cap** punishes HENRYs in high-tax states (NY, CA, NJ, CT)
- Phase-outs eliminate child tax credits, student loan deductions, education credits for HENRYs
- **AMT** (Alternative Minimum Tax) traps high earners with ISOs or high property taxes
- Most HENRYs don't execute: backdoor Roth IRA, mega backdoor Roth 401(k), HSA investing, donor-advised funds, tax-loss harvesting
- A single backdoor Roth strategy executed annually for 20 years at $7K/year = **~$287K** at retirement (tax-free)

### What's Actually Happening
HENRYs know taxes are a problem but treat it as inevitable. They file their returns (often using TurboTax or a basic CPA) but don't do proactive tax *planning*. The difference between tax preparation and tax strategy can be $10K-$30K/year.

Common missed opportunities:
- Not doing backdoor Roth (or not knowing it exists)
- Not maxing HSA or treating it as a spending account instead of investment vehicle
- Not harvesting tax losses in taxable accounts
- Not planning around RSU vesting schedules
- Not bunching charitable contributions via donor-advised funds
- Over-withholding and giving the government a free loan

### What They Need
**Tax-aware guidance** — not tax preparation (TurboTax does that), but strategic recommendations:
- "You're leaving $8,400/year on the table by not doing a backdoor Roth."
- "Your RSU vesting in March will push you into the 35% bracket — consider these offsets."
- "You have $12K in unrealized losses in your taxable account — harvesting now saves $3,600 in taxes."
- A checklist of tax moves, personalized and prioritized by dollar impact.

## Problem 5: Equity Compensation Confusion
**"What do I do with my RSUs / stock options / ESPP?"**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | High (for tech HENRYs — ~40% of the audience) |
| Severity | Very High ($50K-$200K+ in preventable losses or missed optimization) |
| Solvability | High |

### The Evidence
- RSUs are the **largest component of compensation** for most senior tech employees
- Most handle RSU taxation inefficiently, **overpaying by thousands annually** (Austin Creek Capital, 2025)
- Federal supplemental wage withholding is **flat 22%**, but actual marginal rates are **32-37%** — creating surprise tax bills
- Many tech employees hold **40%+ of net worth in employer stock** (concentration risk)
- ISOs vs. NSOs have fundamentally different tax treatments that most employees don't understand
- ESPP discount + holding period rules create optimization opportunities most employees miss

### What's Actually Happening
Tech HENRYs receive $50K-$200K+/year in equity compensation and make one of two mistakes:
1. **Hold everything** (emotional attachment to employer stock → concentration risk)
2. **Sell immediately and don't plan for taxes** (surprise bill in April)

Few do the optimal thing: sell on vest (to diversify), set aside the correct tax amount, and invest the proceeds according to their overall allocation plan.

### What They Need
An **equity comp translator** in plain English:
- Your vesting schedule visualized: what's coming, when, and what it's worth
- Tax impact of each vesting event (at your marginal rate, not the 22% withholding rate)
- Concentration risk score: "67% of your investable assets are in employer stock. If it drops 30%, you lose $180K."
- Clear recommendation: hold vs. sell vs. partial sell, with tax implications
- ESPP guidance: participate at what level, hold or sell

## Problem 6: Competing Priorities — The Allocation Puzzle
**"I have 5 places my money 'should' go. How do I prioritize?"**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Very High (ongoing, every paycheck) |
| Severity | Medium-High ($50K-$200K in long-term opportunity cost from wrong ordering) |
| Solvability | Very High |

### The Evidence
A typical HENRY juggles:
- Student loans: $100K-$250K balance, $800-$2,500/month payments
- Mortgage: $4,000-$8,000/month in major metros
- Childcare: $2,500-$4,000/month per child
- 401(k) contributions (with employer match)
- Backdoor Roth IRA ($7,000/year)
- HSA ($8,300/year for family)
- 529 college savings
- Emergency fund
- Taxable investing
- Extra mortgage payments

Every dollar has 5+ competing claims. The "right" order depends on interest rates, tax brackets, employer matches, and personal goals — but most HENRYs either follow generic advice or just split everything evenly.

### What's Actually Happening
Without a personalized framework, HENRYs make suboptimal allocation decisions:
- Paying extra on a 3% mortgage instead of investing at 7%+ expected return
- Saving for kids' college before maxing their own retirement (put on your own oxygen mask first)
- Holding a massive emergency fund in cash when they have stable dual incomes
- Not capturing the full 401(k) employer match (literally free money)
- Skipping the backdoor Roth because it seems complicated

### What They Need
A **priority engine** — given your specific rates, brackets, and employer benefits, here's the waterfall for your next dollar:

```
1. 401(k) up to employer match → $X,XXX free money/year
2. Pay off debt above 6% interest rate → saves $X,XXX/year
3. Max HSA → $8,300/year, triple tax advantage
4. Max 401(k) → $23,000/year tax-deferred
5. Backdoor Roth IRA → $7,000/year tax-free growth
6. Pay off debt above 4% → saves $X,XXX/year
7. 529 plan → $X,XXX/year for education
8. Taxable investing → remainder
```

Personalized. Specific dollar amounts. Updated when circumstances change.

## Problem 7: The Advice Gap — Nowhere to Turn
**"I need guidance but can't access it."**

| Dimension | Rating |
|---|---|
| Frequency | Constant (background anxiety) |
| Severity | High (compounding cost of delayed or wrong decisions) |
| Solvability | High (this is Henry's core value proposition) |

### The Evidence
- Private wealth management requires **$1M+ minimums** and charges **1% of AUM** ($10K+/year)
- Comprehensive CFP engagement costs **$5K-$15K/year**
- Most financial advisors focus on investment management, not holistic financial planning
- Many push commission-based products (conflicts of interest)
- **25% of HENRYs** aren't sure how their retirement savings are invested
- **80%** are concerned Social Security won't be there for them
- Free tools (Empower, NerdWallet calculators) give generic, depersonalized answers

### What's Actually Happening
HENRYs exist in a no-man's-land:
- **Budgeting apps** assume you don't know where your money goes (too basic)
- **Robo-advisors** give you a generic portfolio and call it "advice" (too passive)
- **Wealth managers** won't talk to you under $1M (too exclusive)
- **Fee-only CFPs** are expensive and slow (scheduled meetings, $5K+/year)
- **Reddit** gives you opinions from strangers who don't know your situation (too risky)
- **Google** gives you generic articles not calibrated to HENRY incomes (too vague)

### What They Need
An **accessible, knowledgeable advisor** that:
- Knows their complete financial picture
- Is available when they have a question (not in 3 weeks)
- Gives specific, personalized, numbers-backed answers
- Doesn't cost $15K/year
- Doesn't push products
- Feels like a trusted, smart friend — not a robot or a salesperson

**This is the core of what Henry provides.**
